Did you know?

The use of the number 60 in time, angle, and (geographic) coordinate measurement was invented by the ancient Babylonians.

But why?

Well, 60 is a “superior highly composite number,” which just means that it has plenty of factors, including the first 6 positive integers: 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, and 6. (Note: factors are a part of number theory math!)
